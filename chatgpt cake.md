# Ingredients (handle with intention)

- **2 cups flour** (sifted like you're separating truth from lies)
- **1 cup sugar** (fine-grained, like the sand in an hourglass you keep ignoring)
- **3 eggs** (preferably stared at for a moment before cracking, just to establish dominance)
- **1/2 cup butter** (melted until it glistens like it has secrets)
- **1 cup milk** (cold... then not cold... you'll understand later)
- **2 tbsp cocoa powder** (dark enough to make you reconsider your life choices)
- **1 tsp vanilla extract** (smells like comfort, behaves like chaos)
- **1 pinch salt** (taken dramatically between two fingers)

# Step 1
**Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).**

Stand in front of it for a second. Let the heat hit your face. This is your preview.

# Step 2
**Mix the dry ingredients.**

Flour, cocoa, salt, into a bowl. Stir slowly, faster, then slow again.
At some point, you will question if stirring differently changes the outcome.
It doesn't. But keep going.

# Step 3
**Butter and sugar.**

In another bowl, pour the melted butter into sugar. Watch it absorb.
It will look wrong at first. It is wrong at first.
Keep stirring until it becomes something unified, like a bad decision you've already committed to.

# Step 4
**Add the eggs.**

Crack the first egg. Stir.
Crack the second egg. Stir harder.
Crack the third egg... hesitate. Why three? Why always three?
Anyway, stir until the mixture becomes glossy and slightly unsettling.

# Step 5
**Milk and vanilla.**

Pour them in. The batter loosens, like it's finally telling you the truth.
At this stage, the smell becomes distracting. Stay focused.

# Step 6
**Combine everything.**

Fold the dry ingredients into the wet.
Not aggressively. Not timidly.
Like you're trying to convince someone to stay without sounding desperate.
The batter should be smooth, thick, and just slightly too shiny.

# Step 7
**Transfer to pan.**

Grease the pan thoroughly. This cake remembers neglect.
Pour the batter in. Tap the pan on the counter once... twice...
Listen closely. That sound? That's air escaping. Or something else. Hard to say.

# Step 8
**Bake for 30–35 minutes.**

Place it in the oven and close the door gently.
Now wait. But not casually. Hover nearby like you're expecting news.
At around 20 minutes, the smell will intensify.
At 25 minutes, you will consider opening the oven.
Don't. This is a test of restraint.

# Final step
**The toothpick test.**

Insert a toothpick into the center.
If it comes out clean, it's done.
If it comes out slightly moist... it's interesting.
Choose your path.

# Cooling (optional, emotionally and physically)
Let the cake rest. It needs to collect itself after what just happened.
Or cut into it immediately and deal with the consequences—crumbs, heat, and mild regret.

# Serving
Eat it alone in the kitchen, standing, at an hour you won't admit to later.
Or share it, but maintain eye contact the entire time. Make it weird.
